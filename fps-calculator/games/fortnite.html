Valorant Performance FAQ

What FPS should I get in Valorant? +

For competitive play, aim for 144+ FPS minimum, ideally 240+ FPS. Most budget GPUs can achieve this on low settings. Professional players typically get 300-500 FPS to minimize input latency.

Frame rate targets:

  • 60 FPS: Playable but not competitive
  • 144 FPS: Good for ranked play
  • 240 FPS: Excellent for competitive
  • 360+ FPS: Professional/top-tier play

Is Valorant more CPU or GPU intensive? +

Valorant is CPU-bound, especially at low graphics settings (which most competitive players use). Your CPU matters more than your GPU for achieving high frame rates.

At 1080p low settings, even a mid-range GPU like RTX 3060 will be limited by your CPU. Invest in a strong CPU (high single-core performance) for maximum FPS.

Best CPUs for Valorant: AMD Ryzen 7 7800X3D, Intel i5-13600K, i7-13700K

Best Valorant settings for maximum FPS? +

Recommended competitive settings:

  • Graphics Quality: Low
  • Material Quality: Low
  • Texture Quality: Medium (minimal FPS impact)
  • Detail Quality: Low
  • UI Quality: Low
  • Vignette: Off
  • VSync: Off (crucial for low latency)
  • Anti-Aliasing: None or MSAA 2x
  • Anisotropic Filtering: 1x
  • Improve Clarity: Off
  • Bloom: Off
  • Distortion: Off

These settings provide +50-100% FPS vs. High/Ultra settings with minimal visual quality loss.

Can my PC run Valorant at 144 FPS? +

Use our calculator above for your specific hardware, but general guidelines:

Minimum for 144+ FPS:

  • GPU: GTX 1050 Ti, RX 570, or better
  • CPU: i3-10100F, Ryzen 3 3100, or better
  • RAM: 8GB minimum (16GB recommended)
  • Settings: Low preset at 1080p

Valorant is very well-optimized. Even budget hardware from 2018-2020 can achieve 144 FPS on competitive settings.

Why is my Valorant FPS low? +

Common causes and fixes:

  • VSync enabled: Turn off in settings (caps FPS to refresh rate)
  • Background apps: Close Chrome, Discord overlay, recording software
  • Power mode: Set Windows to "High Performance" power plan
  • Outdated drivers: Update GPU drivers (NVIDIA/AMD)
  • Thermal throttling: Check CPU/GPU temperatures (should be under 80¬∞C)
  • Wrong GPU: Game running on integrated graphics instead of dedicated GPU
  • RAM speed: Enable XMP in BIOS for rated RAM speed

After fixes, you should see +30-100% FPS improvement.
